## IndiaGate

**Category:** FMCG — Basmati Rice
**Tier:** 1 (AI-gated deliverables — brand rules strictly enforced)
**Slug:** `indiagate`
**Parent company:** KRBL Limited ✓
**Markets:** India + AUS, NZ, USA, Canada, UK, GCC (90+ export countries) ✓

### Brand identity ✓

| Element | Value |
|---|---|
| Primary color | `#1A3C6E` — Navy |
| Secondary color | `#D4AF37` — Gold |
| Accent | `#FFFFFF` — White |
| Warm | `#F5E6C8` — Cream-warm |
| Display font | Playfair Display |
| Body font | Lato |
| Label font | Montserrat |
| Font weights | 400 / 600 / 700 |

### Voice & tone ✓

Quiet, warm, assured. Speaks to the Indian who takes cooking seriously — abroad or at home. Never promotional, never loud. Copy follows an observation → quiet turn → product arrives last structure. Short prose paragraphs with deliberate line breaks. No exclamation marks. No superlatives. The meal arrives at the end of the sentence, not the beginning.

### Brand lines (confirmed active) ✓

| Line | When to lead with it |
|---|---|
| Where Care Meets Craft | Craftsmanship, solo cooking, making something right |
| Everyday Elevated | Daily use, pantry staple, independence |
| Worthy of the Table | Hosting, Iftar, festive, pride after guests leave |
| Expertly Aged | Product authority, elongation demos, chef/quality content |

### Rules (enforced by brand brain) ✓

- **[hard_no]** No discount or price messaging of any kind
- **[hard_no]** No exclamation marks in headlines or body copy
- **[hard_no]** No food superlatives (delicious, yummy, mouth-watering)
- **[visual]** Navy + gold are load-bearing — never swap or desaturate
- **[visual]** No heavy gold theatrics — premium is quiet, not ornate
- **[visual]** Every recipe execution must show grain elongation
- **[tone]** Product arrives last in copy — never lead with it
- **[tone]** Never sound promotional, retail-loud, or like a sale
- **[process]** No more than 2 food-only posts consecutively — mix lifestyle and cultural

### Product lines ✓

- India Gate Classic — aged basmati, premium, hero SKU
- India Gate Mini Mogra — smaller-grain basmati
- India Gate Tibar / Dubar — length-graded variants
- India Gate Feast Rozzana — daily-use
- India Gate Quinoa
- Brown / health variants
- Export packaging: separate designs for AUS/NZ and USA/Canada markets (4–8kg packs)

### Audience ✓

- **Primary:** Indian diaspora abroad — US, Canada, AUS, NZ. 25–45. Built a life overseas. Food is the thing that didn't change. India Gate = consistent, reliable, just right every time.
- **Secondary:** Domestic Indian homemaker — 30–55, SEC A/B, urban, family-first. Hosting is a moment of pride.
- **Tertiary:** Muslim diaspora / Ramadan segment — Iftar hosting, multicultural family tables. Muslim creators + chefs are the right vehicle here.
- **Quaternary:** HoReCa — chefs, restaurants, professional elongation + quality

### Content formats in active use ✓

- AI Storytelling Video (<30s) — split narrative, diaspora emotional content
- AI Product Romance — cinematic grain/steam/pour, no people needed
- Carousel — story builds slide by slide, cultural insights
- Motion Graphics / Typo-led — typography animating over brand palette
- Static — always-on, pantry moment, grocery list content
- Creator-led — Muslim creators + chefs for Ramadan; authority content

### Seasonal calendar anchors ✓

| Pre-Ramadan (early Mar) | Product authority reset | Expertly Aged |
| Ramadan (Mar–Apr) | Iftar hosting, multicultural | Worthy of the Table |

## AlphaKid

**Category:** Kids
**Tier:** 2
**Slug:** `alphakid`

### Brand identity ○

*Full brand pack pending. Add via brand brain after next client meeting.*

### Brand direction (known) ~

- Dual audience: kids 6–12 (the user) + parents 30–45 (the buyer)
- High energy, bright colors, playful tone — content should feel alive
- Educational undertone: parents buy for growth + fun, not just entertainment
- Short-form video performs well; static content needs extra energy to land
- Avoid anything that looks "corporate kids" — polished to the point of feeling manufactured. Needs to feel genuinely playful.

### Voice & tone ~

Two voices needed depending on who's looking:
- **Kids:** Exciting, fast, fun — like a best friend showing you something cool
- **Parents:** Reassuring, developmental framing — "your kid is growing + having fun"

Keep both in mind. Copy that only speaks to parents will bore kids. Copy that only speaks to kids won't convert parents.

### Audience ~

- **Kids (6–12):** The influencer of the purchase decision
- **Parents (30–45):** SEC A/B, values developmental growth alongside fun
- **Platform mix:** Instagram for parents; YouTube/Reels for kids-forward content

## How brand rules get added

1. After any client call, go to **Brands** → [Brand] → **+ Log meeting**
2. Dump raw notes — no structure needed
3. Claude extracts tone rules, visual rules, hard nos, contacts, and tasks
4. Review and confirm — rules accumulate in the brand brain automatically
5. All future task allocations (Allocator) and meeting extractions reference the brain

The brand pages in ChordOS show the accumulated knowledge under **Brand Brain**.

## Adding a new brand

1. Add a row to `brands` table in Supabase: `slug`, `name`, `category`, `tier`, `status = active`
2. Set `account_lead_id` to the relevant person
3. Add `typography`, `colors`, `voice_summary` if you have the brand pack
4. Log the first meeting to kick off the brain
5. Update this file
